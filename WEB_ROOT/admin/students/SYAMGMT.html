The following criteria must be met:

  1. Registered as a Saskatchewan secondary student.
  2. Must be at least 15 years of age.
  3. must meet one of the following criteria:
    • Experience with part-time or seasonal employment
    • Registered in a trade-related Practical and Applied Arts class
    • Registered in a trade-related locally developed course
    • Registered in Career and Work Exploration 10, 20, or 30
    • Registered in a PAA survey course with at least one third trade-related content

Challenge 1 Successful completion of a Practical and Applied Arts credit.
Challenge 2 Research and report on one of the designated trades in Saskatchewan, include the training requirements of the trade selected.
Challenge 3 Completion of Young Worker Readiness Certificate Course.
Challenge 4 Initiate a discussion about apprenticeship and trades careers with an adult in your community.
Challenge 1 Successful completion of a second credit in Practical and Applied Arts.
Challenge 2 Complete an interview with a journeyperson.
Challenge 3 Complete one of the following
  • Design and display a possible career pathway chart for a trade
  • List the four basic steps to obtain journeyperson certification
Challenge 4 Explore a web-based resource connected to apprenticeship.
Challenge 1 Completion of Apprenticeship Terminology.
Challenge 2 Complete at least one of the following intensive work-related experiences:
  • A. spend at least one full day job shadowing a tradesperson.
  • B. spend at least one full day with a community leader (e.g. chief, counsellor, elder’s helper).
  • C. participate in a trade-related work placement for Career and Work Exploration.
Challenge 3 Create a portfolio including apprenticeship related learning.
Challenge 4 Attend a career fair to collect information to compare apprenticeship training with another form of post-secondary opportunity.
